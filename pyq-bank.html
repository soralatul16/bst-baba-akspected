Why PYQs Matter

CBSE repeats question patterns every year. A question that appeared in 2023 will appear again in 2026 — maybe with different company names, but the same concept. Solve all PYQs and you've essentially seen the exam before walking in.

2025 Board3 Marks
State any three characteristics of Principles of Management.
(i) Universal applicability — Apply to all types of organisations.
(ii) Flexible — Can be modified as per situation.
(iii) Cause & Effect — Establish relationship between cause and effect.
2025 Board5 Marks
'A company manufacturing garments has its own fabric division, stitching division and finishing division. Each division has a separate head who is responsible for planning and executing activities of his own division.' Identify the principle of management followed here and explain it.
Unity of Direction — Each division has one head and one plan for its group of activities. This principle states that activities with the same objective should have one head and one plan, ensuring coordinated and focused effort.
2024 Board4 Marks
Distinguish between 'Unity of Command' and 'Unity of Direction'.
(1) Meaning: One employee, one superior vs One head, one plan for activities.
(2) Purpose: Prevents confusion vs Prevents repetition.
(3) Relates to: People vs Activities.
(4) Outcome: Individual effectiveness vs Group coordination.
2024 Board1 Mark
Fayol's principle that suggests maintaining a chain of superiors from the highest to the lowest is called:
Scalar Chain.
2023 Board5 Marks
'In a factory, weights of different articles are measured, movement of workers is studied, and exact time to complete a task is determined.' Identify the techniques of Scientific Management.
1. Standardisation — Measuring weights to set consistent standards.
2. Motion Study — Studying movement to eliminate wasteful motions.
3. Time Study — Determining exact time for efficiency planning.
2023 Board3 Marks
State the principle of management that requires a manager to be kind, fair and just to the subordinates.
Equity. This principle states that management should treat employees with fairness and justice, without discrimination or favouritism.
Most Repeated Patterns

Fayol vs Taylor (4 marks) — appeared 8 out of 10 years. Case study identifying principles (5 marks) — every single year. Unity of Command vs Unity of Direction (4 marks) — 7 out of 10 years. Learn these three and you've covered 12+ guaranteed marks.
