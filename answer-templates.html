How to Write Answers That Score Full Marks

📋 Format for every mark type ✅ Model answers included 🎯 Board examiner approved
The #1 Reason Students Lose Marks

It's not because they don't know the content — it's because they don't format their answers correctly. A 3-mark answer needs exactly 3 points. A 5-mark case study needs identification + definition + application. Follow these templates and you'll never lose marks on structure again.

1 Mark MCQ / One-Word / Fill in the Blank

Format: Direct answer. No explanation needed. One line only.
Q: Who is known as the Father of Scientific Management?
A: F.W. Taylor.

3 Marks Short Answer (50–75 words)

Format: Definition (1 line) + 3 brief points OR 1 para explanation. Write exactly 3 points for 3 marks.
Q: State any three principles of management given by Fayol.

A:
(i) Division of Work — Work should be divided into small tasks assigned to specialists for greater efficiency.
(ii) Unity of Command — An employee should receive orders from one boss only to avoid confusion.
(iii) Equity — Management should treat all employees fairly and justly, without discrimination.
Scoring Tip

Each point = 1 mark. Write the principle name in bold, then a one-line explanation. Don't write paragraphs — examiners scan for keywords.

4 Marks Distinguish Between (150 words)

Format: Table with 4 points of difference. Always include a "Basis" column. Each row = 1 mark.
Q: Distinguish between Fayol and Taylor.

BasisFayolTaylor
Known asFather of General ManagementFather of Scientific Management
FocusTop-level managementShop-floor / worker level
BasisPersonal experienceExperiments & observation
ApplicabilityUniversalMainly factories
Scoring Tip

Always use a table format for "distinguish between" questions. Examiners can mark it faster and you're less likely to miss a point. Write exactly 4 rows for 4 marks.

5 Marks Case Study (150–200 words)

Format: Identify concept (1 mark) → Define/State it (1 mark) → Quote from the case (1 mark) → Explain the application (2 marks)
Q: "Rajesh, a manager at XYZ Ltd, ensures that every worker reports to him only and does not take instructions from any other manager."
Identify the principle of Fayol.

A:
The principle identified is Unity of Command. (1 mark — identification)

According to this principle, an employee should receive orders from one and only one superior. (1 mark — definition)

In the given case, Rajesh ensures "every worker reports to him only and does not take instructions from any other manager." (1 mark — quoting case)

This avoids confusion, conflicting instructions, and divided loyalty. When workers report to a single boss, accountability is clear and work is carried out more efficiently. (2 marks — explanation)
Scoring Tip

Always quote the case study. Write the exact words from the question in quotes. This shows the examiner you've actually read and applied the case, not just written a textbook answer.

6 Marks Long Answer (200 words)

Format: Opening line (definition) + 6 points with one-line explanation each. Or 3 points with 2-line explanation each.
Q: Explain any four principles of management given by Fayol.

A:
Henri Fayol, the Father of General Management, gave 14 principles based on his personal experience. Four key principles are:

(i) Division of Work: Total work should be divided into small tasks assigned to specialists. This leads to specialisation and increased productivity.

(ii) Authority & Responsibility: Authority is the right to give orders; responsibility is the obligation to complete the task. Both must go hand in hand.

(iii) Unity of Command: Every employee should receive orders from one superior only. Dual subordination leads to confusion and undermines discipline.

(iv) Esprit De Corps: Management should promote team spirit and unity among employees. 'Union is strength' — collective effort benefits the organisation.
Scoring Tip

For 6-mark questions asking "explain any four," write 4 points × 1.5 marks each. Bold the key term, define it in one line, then add one line of explanation or example. Don't write 6 shallow points — 4 well-explained ones score higher.

General Rules
Golden Rules of BM Answer Writing
  • Match points to marks. 3 marks = 3 points. 4 marks = 4 points. Never write 2 points for a 4-mark question.
  • Bold your keywords. Underline or bold principle names, key terms, and definitions. Examiners scan for these.
  • Use numbered points, not paragraphs. (i), (ii), (iii) is easier to mark than a wall of text.
  • Quote the case study in case-study questions. Use the exact words from the question.
  • Use tables for "distinguish between" questions. Always.
  • Don't overwrite. A precise 3-line answer scores more than a vague 10-line one. Quality over quantity.
  • Start with the keyword. Don't build up to the answer — state it first, then explain.
